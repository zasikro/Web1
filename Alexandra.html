CHERRY BLOSSOM TREES

Cherry blossoms are delicate pink or white flowers that bloom in spring, symbolizing beauty, renewal, and the fleeting nature of life. They are most famously associated with Japan, where they are celebrated during the Hanami festival. Their brief but stunning bloom creates breathtaking landscapes, attracting visitors worldwide. Cherry blossoms belong to the Rosaceae family, which is the rose family. Within this family, they are part of the Prunus genus, which includes cherries, plums, peaches, apricots, and almonds. The most famous cherry blossom species is Prunus serrulata, commonly known as the Japanese cherry or sakura.Other members of the Rosaceae family share similar characteristics, such as five-petaled flowers, often with a pleasant fragrance and a close relationship with fruit-bearing trees. There are 3 types of blossom; cherry blossom, plum blossom and peach blossom.

HISTORY OF CHERRY BLOSSOMS

Before the prehistoric era, cherry blossoms traveled throughout Japan after migrating there some thousands of years ago. Because of decades of hybridization, there are currently over 300 different types of sakura, or cherry blossom, trees in Japan. According to early accounts, sakura celebrations gained popularity during the Heian Period (794–1185), when emperors and Imperial Palace officials started holding feasts beneath the tree's blossoming branches. Through the samurai, the festivities spread to the general populace, and the parties gained widespread popularity throughout all societal classes in Japan. The country's traditions have remained essentially unaltered over the centuries, and the tree is still revered as a significant cultural icon. It cannot be overstated that in Japan, the sakura is serious business -- even government business. Each year, the blossom forecast is analyzed, predicted and released to the public by the Japan Meteorological Agency.

JAPANS MOST POPULAR FLOWER

Tucked away in the bustling city of Tokyo, Shinjuku Gyoen is a true gem for anyone looking to soak up the stunning spectacle of cherry blossoms. This sprawling garden covers 58.3 hectares (133 acres) and wraps around a 3.5-kilometer path. It's home to around 1,100 Japanese cherry trees that come alive with color each spring. One of the garden’s most distinctive features is its diverse collection of cherry tree varieties, each blooming at different times. This staggered blooming period allows visitors to enjoy an extended cherry blossom season, making Shinjuku Gyoen an exceptional destination for hanami enthusiasts. Beyond the blossoms, the park’s shops offer a selection of seasonal sweets and souvenirs inspired by cherry blossoms, providing visitors with the opportunity to take home a piece of the season.
